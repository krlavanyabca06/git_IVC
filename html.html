What is Computer Science?

Computer science (CS) is the scientific and practical approach to computation and its applications. It involves the study of algorithms, data structures, software development, hardware systems, and theoretical foundations such as computation theory and logic.

Key branches include:

  • Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning
  • Software Engineering
  • Computer Systems & Architecture
  • Human-Computer Interaction
  • Data Science & Big Data

Why study CS? Graduates are equipped to build the technologies that power modern life, enjoy high employability, and can work in virtually any industry. CS fosters problem‑solving, logical thinking, and creativity.
